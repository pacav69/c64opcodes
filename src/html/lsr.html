LSR

Shift right one bit, memory or accumulator

Operation: 0 => 7 6 5 4 3 2 1 0 => C



Mode Syntax HEX LEN TIME
Accumulator LSR $4A 1 2
Zero Page LSR $44 $46 2 5
Zero Page,X LSR $44,X $56 2 6
Absolute LSR $4400 $4E 3 6
Absolute,X LSR $4400,X $5E 3 7

The negative status flag is always unconditionally cleared.
The zero flag is cleared unless the operand is zero.
The carry flag is copied from the least significant bit (bit 0) of the operand prior to shifting.

Displaces the bits of the accumulator or a memory address specified by the operand, one position to the right. The rightmost bit goes to the Carry flag (C). From the left side, zeros are entering and N flag that controls the Negative bit flag (bit 7) will be always at zero when we make a lsr.

It's the inverse operation of the instruction ASL.

Making an LSR, is equivalent to performing a division by 2. This detail is exploited to perform quick divisions, always that the divisor is a power of 2.

It's remarkable to note here that the 6502 (and 6510) does not have any instruction to perform divisions.
